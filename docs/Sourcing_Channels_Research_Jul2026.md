## 1. Boardwave — Rank 1

**What it is:** Free, curated community of 2,000+ European software founders/CEOs/Chairs/NEDs, founded April 2022 by Phill Robinson (ex-Exact CEO). Social enterprise — profits to Cure Parkinson's. UK-centric (London HQ, flagship events in London), expanding across 15 countries.

**Why it fits:** Full membership requires €1M–€100M+ ARR — your £2.5–10M sweet spot sits in the thickest part of that range. Bootstrapped founders are explicitly welcomed, and free membership specifically attracts founders who won't pay for YPO-style clubs. The **Connection Centre** (searchable directory of all members, filterable by growth stage, role, country) is the single most origination-relevant asset, and partner access to it is an explicit benefit.

**Access:** Investors cannot join as members — they join the **Investment Partner programme** (fee not public; contact partnerships@boardwave.org). Partners get Connection Centre access, dinner/event attendance, sponsorship and content slots.

**Red flags:**
- ~40 investor partners already, including direct competitors for the same deals: Hg (strategic partner), Livingbridge, August Equity, FPE Capital, GCP, BGF, Oakley, Verdane, AshGrove, Axiom Equity.
- Explicit "Sales and Recruitment Free Zone" code of conduct — solicitation risks removal. Origination must be relationship-led.
- Culture celebrates *scaling to €100M*, not selling — frame as growth partnership, not buyout.
- Ireland presence appears thin (no dedicated chapter found).

**Tactic:** Join as Investment Partner; differentiate through genuine mentoring (575-mentor programme) and content on "what PE actually pays for £2.5–10M software companies" rather than another logo on the dinner circuit.

## 2. SaaS Communities — Rank 2 (selective)

The generic "SaaS Slack community" label hides huge variance. Three are worth real money and time; most are not.

### MicroConf Connect — purest bootstrapped fit
- ~300 vetted bootstrapped SaaS founders, pre-revenue to $10M ARR; $49/mo. At MicroConf Europe 2025, 30% of attendees were at ≥$100K MRR (~$1.2M+ ARR).
- Investors are explicitly admitted in small numbers as **Verified Partner Members** (marked profile, strict no-pitch, cold DMs are a ban offence). Member directory filterable by revenue stage and region.
- **Tactic:** Verified Partner membership + sponsor MicroConf Europe 2026 (Reykjavik, 21–23 Sept, sponsors@microconf.com). Small absolute numbers but every member matches the bootstrapped profile.

### Helm (formerly The Supper Club) — best UK-native fit
- 400+ UK scale-up founders with a hard **£2M+ revenue floor** — exactly your demographic, though sector-agnostic, not SaaS-only. Founders-only with strict no-sales ethos; investors can't join. Engage via event sponsorship/partnerships and member referrals.

### SaaStock (Dublin) — the Ireland play
- The largest UK/Ireland SaaS founder gathering, with a dedicated investor track — investors expected and welcome. SaaStock Founder Membership targets founders scaling to $10M ARR. **Tactic:** investor pass + hosted side-dinner for bootstrapped founders at $3M+ ARR. Notably, even SaaStr points to SaaStock as *the* Europe-focused SaaS event.

### Worth watching, not prioritising
- **TinySeed / SaaS Institute** ($1M+ ARR founders): closed ecosystem; engage as LP or as a named exit route for their EMEA portfolio.
- **SaasRise**: $1M–100M ARR CEO community ($197/mo) with member directory — right revenue band, but US-skewed.
- **Pavilion (London chapter), RevGenius, Modern Sales Pros, Exit Five**: members are executives/marketers, not owners — useful for market intelligence and operating-partner sourcing only.
- **Indie Hackers, Trends.vc, Furlough, SaaS Growth Hacks (FB)**: sub-£1M skew, too early.
- No large dedicated "UK bootstrapped SaaS Slack" exists — UK bootstrappers cluster in MicroConf, Helm, Boardwave and SaaStock's orbit.

**Universal red flag:** every quality community is explicitly anti-solicitation. This channel is a 12–24 month reputation build (content, AMAs, sponsorship), not an outreach volume play.

## 3. Tech Nation Successor Bodies — Rank 3

**What happened:** Tech Nation lost its government grant to Barclays Eagle Labs (Jan 2023), closed, and was acquired by **Founders Forum Group** (April 2023). It now runs commercially: Future Fifty, Upscale, Climate, Libra, Creo, DeepTech, plus pitch competitions — and won back the Global Talent Visa endorsement role from August 2025.

**The useful assets:**
- **Future Fifty alumni lists (2013–2026, fully public).** Entry bar: Series B raised, £5M+ revenue or 50% YoY growth. Current cohorts are unicorn-track VC companies (wrong profile), but **older cohorts contain plateaued companies at £5–15M revenue with tired cap tables** — prime secondary/buyout candidates. Highest signal-to-noise item in this whole category.
- **Barclays Eagle Labs Demo Directory** — free investor registration, filterable matching platform drawing on Barclays' business-banking base, i.e. it captures **revenue-generating founder-owned regional businesses**, not just VC-track startups. £1.67bn in capital requests over 2 years; expanded to strategic investors Jan 2026. Skews early, but it's free.
- **Upscale alumni** (avg £3M+ ARR at entry) enter your band 1–2 years post-programme — but eligibility requires prior VC/angel money, so pure bootstrappers are structurally excluded.
- **ScaleUp Institute** — data/mapping (44,595 UK scaleups, regional indices), not a name-by-name pipeline.

**Red flag:** the entire programme stack defines eligibility in VC-round terms. This channel yields *founder-led-but-VC-backed* targets needing a secondaries angle, not bootstrapped ones.

## 4. Entrepreneur First Alumni — Rank 4

**The pool:** 600+ companies created since 2011; roughly 350–400 formed 2012–2018 (now 8–14 years old), overwhelmingly London-based in those vintages. The **portfolio directory is fully public and filterable** by location, industry, founding year and stage — a free screening list.

**The problems:**
- ~90% never reached Series A (Sifted/Dealroom, 2021) — most are dead, not profitable plateaus. Realistic pool of survivors at £2.5–10M revenue: **perhaps 20–40 companies**.
- Every alumnus has EF (and usually seed VCs) on the cap table — the "bootstrapped" screen fails by construction. "Founder-led" usually still holds.
- Heavy deep-tech/AI skew; only ~213 of 645 tagged SaaS.
- **No documented EF-alumni PE buyout exists** — all found exits were trade sales (Twitter, Apple, Spotify, Moody's). You'd be pioneering, not following a pattern.
- Post-2023 cohorts incorporate as US Delaware C-Corps — new vintages are increasingly not UK companies.

**Tactic:** one-off screening exercise, not a channel. Filter directory to 2014–2018 London B2B software, cross-reference against Companies House via SmartFill, approach as founder-led secondaries (EF and seed funds may welcome liquidity). Candidates worth screening: Chattermill, zeroheight, Flexciton, nPlan, Mimica, Metomic, Genie AI (revenues unverified — run them through SmartFill).

## 5. SaaStr — Rank 5

**What it is now:** Rebranded "SaaStr AI"; flagship Annual in SF Bay Area (10,000+ attendees, May 2026). SaaStr Europa is effectively dead as a brand — replaced by a smaller **SaaStr AI London** (2,500 attendees, Dec 2025); a Dec 2026 London edition is *likely but unconfirmed*.

**Why it ranks last for this thesis:**
- Audience is overwhelmingly **VC-track**: content framed "$0 to $100M ARR," 800+ VCs at Annual 2025, all investor infrastructure built for venture fundraising. EBITDA is essentially absent from its content taxonomy. A control-buyout message is counter-cultural.
- US-centric; European footprint shrinking (Europa 2024: ~3,500 → London 2025: 2,500) with continuity risk.
- The AI-pivot pulls the audience *earlier* stage (SaaStr Fund thesis: $100K–$2M ARR).
- Cost vs UK deal flow: Annual ≈ $1,700/ticket + US travel for a mostly-US crowd. Poor cost-per-relevant-conversation vs SaaStock Dublin.

**Tactic if used at all:** one partner attends SaaStr AI London each December (~£2–4K all-in): braindates on "exit options for profitable SaaS founders," a mentor slot, one hosted dinner. No sponsorship.

## Overall Recommendations

1. **Spend the real money on Boardwave partnership + MicroConf Verified Partner + SaaStock Dublin presence.** These three cover: UK software CEOs at scale (Boardwave), pure bootstrappers (MicroConf), and Ireland (SaaStock).
2. **Mine the free public lists now:** Future Fifty alumni 2014–2020 cohorts + EF portfolio 2014–2018 London B2B software → upload to the tool and run SmartFill for CH financials and fit scores. Zero cost, immediate pipeline.
3. **Register free on Barclays Eagle Labs Demo Directory** as an investor — the only channel with founder-owned regional business coverage.
4. **Skip SaaStr sponsorship entirely;** send one person to the London event if it's confirmed for Dec 2026.
5. **Common trap across all channels:** anti-solicitation norms. Build the "PE for bootstrapped founders" content position once and reuse it everywhere — benchmarks, exit-anatomy AMAs, valuation guides for £2.5–10M SaaS.
